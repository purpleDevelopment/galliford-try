About this app

The Honeywell Installer Assistant has been designed to help you successfully install the Honeywell Sundial RF² Heating packs, the ST9000 range of timers and programmers, or the CM900 range of programmable thermostats. It has information that helps you in a number of areas

  1. How to ensure that you can easily set up the controls so that they reflect the choices your customers has made when it comes to choosing a variety of features i.e. 12 or 24 hour clock or how many timing periods they require. This App gives you easy menu choices which are then automatically translated into information on what parameters you need to change within the Installer Set Up area. This information can then be emailed to you and your customer for future reference
  2. Where applicable, depending on the model, there is an area that explains how you can set up a service reminder. You can then email the details that you have set to yourself and your customer for future reference
  3. A knowledge base that contains the basic technical information you need to install the Honeywell ST9000 products you have chosen. This includes dependent on product and model:
    1. A pictorial overview of the controls and the functions of each button
    2. Schematic diagrams (Sundial RF² only)
    3. Wiring diagrams
    4. Replacement wiring information
    5. Advice on binding and re-binding the wireless components
    6. A commissioning checklist and a completion checklist
    7. Advice on positioning the units and how to carry out signal strength tests
    8. A Find a merchant feature that is searchable by postcode or current location. The merchants listed on this feature have a wide range of Honeywell products always in stock
  4. A link to the Honeywell UK website for further information

Q. I can't see the Sundial RF² Pack, Honeywell ST9000 or CM900 product I require on the front screen?
A. Make sure you select the product you need information for by swiping in the main box at the top of the front screen.

Q. How do I record the details of the job that I am working on so I can identify the customer on the emails I send?
A. On the front screen there is a field that can be filled in with your own details such as name of customer and job number. Whatever is inserted into this field will be entered onto all the emails you send UNTIL YOU OVERWRITE THIS INFORMATION.

Q. How do I enter the Installer mode to change the parameters?
A. At the top of the screen that contains the parameters you can toggle to an area that has written instructions. Please only access this area if you understand the functions of the programmer and understand how the choices you will be making will change the way the programmer or timer operates.

Q. Do I have to email the details of the information collected to myself?
A. No, we have added this as an extra feature to help you communicate what you have done to the heating system to your customer and also keep a record of the settings yourself. However, you do not need to use the email feature if you do not want to.

Q. I do not have any 3G or wireless access and I want to clear the data in the App and carry out another install – how do I save the data entered?
A. Use your smartphone or tablets save draft email feature to save the emails to send when you have wifi or 3g access.

Q. I already have your Wiring Guide App, are the wiring diagrams for these products the same as the diagrams in the Wiring Guide App?
A. Yes, we have included the diagrams in this app so that you can access this information all from the one guide.

Q. Are the Sundial RF², Honeywell ST9000 and CM900 Installation Guides and User Guides included within this App?
A. Installation Guides and User Guides are available for the CM Range of programmable thermostats. However, there are links through to the Honeywell UK website and all the user guides can be found by searching for the product you require.

Q. I cant email the completion or commissioning checklists to myself or my customer? (Sundial RF² & ST9000 only)
A. Please make sure you have completed all the boxes before trying to send the email.
