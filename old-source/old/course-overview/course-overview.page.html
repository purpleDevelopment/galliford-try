Course Organisation

All CBAB training is organised and coordinated through local HR training coordinators.

The Foundation Course is available to all personnel via the Challenging Beliefs, Affecting Behaviour website. This provides an overview of the Challenging Beliefs, Affecting Behaviour programme to all.

There are three main training courses thereafter:

  • The Leadership Course
  • The Leadership Update
  • Coaching Course.

The Leadership course is the bedrock of the Challenging Beliefs, Affecting Behaviour training programme.

This course is aimed at front line ‘Leaders’ of the business who manage people and/or processes. This typically includes junior management up to and including Executive Directors. All departments are covered by this training.

The course covers the psychology of why people act the way they do, offers an explanation as to how some accidents occur, looks at real safety leadership and introduces the ‘concept of care’ which underpins the programme. Furthermore, the course provides an introduction to the ten Challenging Beliefs, Affecting Behaviour engagement tools that form the toolkit.

The Coaching Course is held for carefully selected members of staff who their Coach Champion and fellow Directors believe have the attributes and influence to become a site Coach. For the role to be fully effective, careful selection is is paramount.

Typical attendees are from front line Operations roles such as operatives, Foremen, Assistant Site Manager, etc. but the course is open to all selected staff in all departments. This course also includes the psychology explained in Leadership for New Starters but covers the ten engagement tools in greater working detail. Throughout the course, delegates will create a Coach Plan which is bespoke to them and their current site. The courses are scheduled for 30 persons using external venues.

Delegates are not required to have attended a Leadership for New Starters course prior to this one.

Ahead of the course, all potential delegates will be contacted by a Health, Safety and Sustainability Advisor who will explain the role and responsibilities in more detail. After the course, the new Coach will receive a follow-up visit from a Health, Safety and Sustainability Advisor which allows them to discuss any issues or concerns they may have.
