Leadership will ensure success

All businesses are expected to take up the Challenging Beliefs, Affecting Behaviour programme. The energy and support of the senior management of the Business Units will be reflected in the level of measurable success.

All divisions are expected to proactively appoint the necessary time and resource to deliver the Challenging Beliefs, Affecting Behaviour programme including:

Divisional and Board Support ensure that the uptake and implementation of Challenging Beliefs, Affecting Behaviour is discussed, measured and supported as part of routine business planning.
Appointment of Coach Champions — All business units will appoint a Coach Champion. He/she is a senior member of the business unit Leadership Team - typically an Operations Director or similar.
Appointment of Site Coaches — ensure sufficient number and quality of site coaches are appointed to support delivery of the Challenging Beliefs, Affecting Behaviour programme at site level and to develop and maintain site specific coach plans.
Site Coach Plan — ensure an appropriate and bespoke Coach Plan is designed, maintained and implemented at all work locations.
Operational Teams - should ensure that site activities are planned to support the Challenging Beliefs, Affecting Behaviour initiative; for example, by programming time for Worker Engagement Forums, First Ten Minutes Briefings and arranging the appropriate level of training and induction.
Sub-contractors and Suppliers — The support and active involvement of all our subcontractors and suppliers is pivotal to the success of this initiative. Hence, all contractors and suppliers should be made aware of the Challenging Beliefs, Affecting Behaviour Safety Leadership Programme and of the requirement for them to fully embrace this initiative. This will include making their staff aware of the requirement to take part in and support various worker engagement forums and other behavioural safety initiatives. The Tender Enquiry Documents (TED's) and Pre-Order Meeting Minutes (POMM's) both contain requirements for the supply chain to participate in Challenging Beliefs, Affecting Behaviour.
Appendix 3 includes a sample letter to a subcontractor.
All personnel — all working practices should be carried out in a climate where safety, the concept of care and our safety vision are paramount. Workers should be actively encouraged to intervene in a positive manner with their colleagues, other contractors and superiors to prevent an unsafe act or condition. Various tools to assist in this process are referred to in the Communication and Implementation Tools section of this document which begins on page 11.

Training — ensure all personnel (new starters, coaches, leaders, contractors) are implemented in line with the strategic plan - see section [X] on training link.

All Business Units will be supported by the Group HS&E Support Team, who will be responsible for the upkeep of the Toolkit, the strategic management of the training elements, and for co-ordinating and sharing best practice in implementation.

As part of the wider Challenging Beliefs, Affecting Behaviour Programme Review, the Toolkit is updated on a regular basis and amended to reflect new initiatives, Safety Climate Surveys and feedback from worker engagement forums. The Behavioural Programme Review is undertaken on a regular basis by senior managers from all Divisions, the HS&E Support Team, and external partners as appropriate. Appendix 1 includes a list of Review Team members and a sample agenda.

Implementation of CBAB will be supported by the HS&S support team who are also responsible for the upkeep of the toolkit, the strategic management of the training elements, and for coordinating and sharing best practice and implementation.

As part of the wider CBAB programme review, the toolkit will be continually updated to reflect new initiatives, performance review and feedback from worker engagement forums.

A senior leadership team review will be undertaken on a regular basis with representatives of all businesses, senior representatives and the HS&S support team.
