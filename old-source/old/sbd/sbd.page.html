Safe Behaviour Discussions (SBDs)

SBDs are used by management to promote and maintain safe behaviours on site by repeatedly engaging with operatives to correct poor practice and reaffirm positive behaviours.

Without such continued attention and safety leadership, operational staff are likely to lapse into bad habits, inappropriate shortcuts and unsafe behaviours. This isn’t with the intent of putting themselves or others at risk, but in the absence of management safety input, they are forced to make their own decisions, which can unknowingly result in negative consequences.

Short and frequent conversations with operational staff help to maintain their awareness of safety and the risks around them but, more importantly, reinforce their behaviour when they are commended and complimented for working in a safe manner. Such ‘positive interventions’ are becoming increasingly common in our industry, and the Safe Behaviour Discussion is Galliford Try’s response to this matter.
